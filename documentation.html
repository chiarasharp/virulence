Documentation



Building a theory


Why did COVID-19 spread so rapidly in Italy, making us reach one of the highest death rates in the world? There was the need to look at the conditions that made it possible, gathering information through data and articles from multiple sources. It is often demonstrated that the most relevant events may originate from the most unexpected causes. We will indeed have the chance to look at them carefully. Now we are gonna discuss the many aspects taken into consideration during the research. The following list has been considered for all the 20 italian regions:


  • Air pollution due to PM10 (data files here)
  • Winter temperatures
  • Mobility (data files here)
  • Density and age of population (data files here)
  • Density of health facilities

Issues analysis


This research took into consideration several aspects to get a clear view of the pandemic's spread mechanisms: it was in fact crucial to list all the steps, for instance the analysis of economy, ethics, laws and many other traits of a country, Italy in our case. These steps have been decribed carefully in the documents section, to provide info about the considered data.

Data mashup


The datasets used for this project have been merged together in order to get a clear idea of how the social aspects described in the datasets were related to each other.

Scanning


Once the mashup had been completed, the focus moved to political, economical and public/private health aspects, all sharing a crucial role in the COVID-19 spread

Sharing results


The main purpose of this project is to deliver data and explain complex concepts to users in an understandable way. That's why the website includes visualisations of maps and graphs that can be configured by users themselves, in order to let them look at interesting datasets selectively.

Looking for information


The many sources that allowed us to know more about the topic are listed below:


  • WHO

    : the specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health

  • ISTAT

    : the main producer of official statistics in Italy

  • Ministero della salute

    : the italian ministry of health. It has duties in health matters, prophylaxis and relationship with international and european institutions in the field of health

  • Il Foglio

    : an italian daily newspaper, founded in 1996 by the politician and journalist Giuliano Ferrara

  • The Local

    : an english-language digital news publisher with local editions in Austria, Denmark, France, Germany, Italy, Norway, Spain, Sweden and Switzerland. Each site has separate editorial teams, each focusing on its respective market

  • The New York Times

    : the american daily newspaper founded in 1851 and based in New York, with readers all over the world

  • OnData

    : an association that promotes the opening of public data to make it accessible to everybody
